## Captured Source

> Noor Aram had three names in the city records.
>
> The first was the name her mother gave her, written in blue ink on a kitchen
> wall the year before the floods took the lower quarter.
>
> The second was the name the Restoration Census assigned her when the wall was
> recovered from river silt and matched to a child with no surviving district.
>
> The third was the name she would receive if the Sarqel Trust accepted her as
> heir.
>
> She had spent most of her life avoiding the third.
>
> At 06:12, the Dead Exchange found her anyway.
>
> Her wrist seal turned gold while she was buying tea from a stall under the
> western viaduct. The vendor saw the color before she did and stepped back from
> the counter. People always stepped back from gold. Gold meant court process,
> estate gravity, old money waking in public.
>
> Noor covered the seal with her sleeve.
>
> Too late.
>
> A street lens unfolded from a lamp post and read her face. Three delivery
> drones paused midair, recalculated liability, and drifted away. The tea vendor
> began wiping the same metal cup with increasing devotion.
>
> "I don't consent to service," Noor said.
>
> The lamp post chimed.
>
> ```text
> SERVICE IS NOTICE ONLY
> ```
>
> "That is what people say when they are about to ruin you."
>
> The summons opened across her palm.
>
