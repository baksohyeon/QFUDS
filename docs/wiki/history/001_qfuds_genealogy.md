# QFUDS Idea Genealogy

## Status Boundary

This file is a repository-history map. It is not a roadmap, result document, or
new theory note. Current project status remains governed by
[docs/05_next_steps/000_roadmap.md](../../05_next_steps/000_roadmap.md).

Current retained-branch classification:
Phenomenological IV/IDE; Not Yet A Physical Model.

This classification applies to the current retained branch only. It does not
define the entire QFUDS research program.

Level 2B remains blocked. Retained `Gamma(a)` is a prototype phenomenological
transfer profile, not a derived physical law.

## Reading Rule

Read each stage as provenance and narrowing history. The purpose is to explain
what the repository preserved, demoted, rejected, or left open. Do not read the
sequence as proof that the early motivation physically derives the current
retained branch.

Each stage records:

- idea introduced;
- problem it tried to solve;
- what survived;
- what was demoted;
- what was rejected;
- what remains active.

## Chronological Genealogy

The repository history narrowed along this path:

```text
Landauer trigger
-> information conservation
-> black-hole information question
-> white-hole / remnant intuition
-> quantum foam medium intuition
-> dark matter / dark energy unification attempt
-> minimal two-phase dark sector
-> Gamma(a) transfer prototype
-> collapsed-fraction / information-production proxy
-> phenomenological IV/IDE demotion
-> blocked Physical-QFUDS Level 2B
```

This path records how questions changed. It does not make the early motivations
physical evidence for the retained branch.

## Landauer Principle

- idea introduced: information erasure has a thermodynamic cost.
- problem it tried to solve: why information should be treated as physical
  rather than only bookkeeping.
- what survived: Landauer remains the origin trigger for the information and
  entropy questions.
- what was demoted: Landauer is motivation, not a cosmological source law.
- what was rejected: no claim that Landauer alone derives dark matter, dark
  energy, `Gamma(a)`, or phase transfer.
- what remains active: provenance for why the project asks information-flow
  questions.

## Information Conservation

- idea introduced: information should not simply disappear in a unitary physical
  description.
- problem it tried to solve: how black-hole evaporation can preserve information
  while making recovery practically difficult.
- what survived: the distinction between destroyed information and inaccessible
  scrambled information.
- what was demoted: information conservation is not treated as a direct
  dark-sector equation.
- what was rejected: no repository result shows information conservation
  produces vacuum-pressure phase B.
- what remains active: a constraint on future source ideas, if they try to use
  information processing as `X`.

## Black-Hole Information Paradox

- idea introduced: black holes are extreme information-processing systems.
- problem it tried to solve: where information goes when matter enters black
  holes and Hawking radiation later leaves.
- what survived: black-hole entropy, mass growth, and strong-gravity histories
  remain possible future source-audit quantities.
- what was demoted: black-hole language is candidate-source motivation, not an
  accepted QFUDS source.
- what was rejected: no accepted equation maps black-hole information
  compression into smooth phase-B vacuum pressure.
- what remains active: black-hole-coupled source audits are safe only as audits
  unless all admission-rule items are supplied.

## White-Hole And Remnant Intuition

- idea introduced: time-reversed or delayed-release channels, including
  white-hole-like remnants, might preserve information.
- problem it tried to solve: how information could be stored or returned after
  black-hole evaporation.
- what survived: remnants remain optional defect-sector or DM-only audit
  candidates.
- what was demoted: white-hole and remnant language is provenance unless it
  supplies abundance, stress-energy, release rate, and observational tests.
- what was rejected: remnants are not a current dark-energy source and do not
  define `Q^nu`, `delta Q`, or phase-B pressure.
- what remains active: remnant-sector work is safe only as DM-only or
  defect-sector audit unless smooth `w ~= -1` stress-energy is derived.

## Quantum Foam Intuition

- idea introduced: the dark sector might be an effective macroscopic behavior of
  quantum spacetime foam.
- problem it tried to solve: whether dark matter and dark energy could be two
  infrared manifestations of one underlying spacetime sector.
- what survived: quantum foam remains the broad model language for the QFUDS
  research question.
- what was demoted: generic foam language is not physical evidence.
- what was rejected: no microscopic foam degree of freedom, action, stress
  tensor, or perturbation prescription has been derived.
- what remains active: the broader DM-to-DE phase-transition hypothesis remains
  open but unsupported.

## Dark Matter And Dark Energy Unification Attempt

- idea introduced: phase A clusters like pressureless matter and phase B is
  smooth with vacuum-like pressure.
- problem it tried to solve: whether one effective sector can reproduce behavior
  normally attributed to cold dark matter and dark energy.
- what survived: two-phase bookkeeping remains useful and has an LCDM null
  limit.
- what was demoted: the retained branch is not a physical unification theory.
- what was rejected: the repository does not support presenting the retained
  branch as novel physical QFUDS.
- what remains active: phenomenological IV/IDE work can continue if it is
  labeled as phenomenology.

## Two-Phase Dark Sector Model

- idea introduced: background equations for phase A, phase B, and a transfer
  profile `Gamma(a)`.
- problem it tried to solve: make the broad intuition explicit enough to test
  against LCDM and known interacting dark-sector models.
- what survived: the background equations and null limit are useful test
  scaffolding.
- what was demoted: with free `Gamma(a)`, the model belongs near interacting
  dark energy or interacting vacuum.
- what was rejected: background survival does not establish perturbation, CMB,
  matter-power, or survey-likelihood viability.
- what remains active: the two-phase language remains a bookkeeping layer for
  controlled audits.

## Gamma(a) Prototype

- idea introduced: a transfer profile controls phase-A to phase-B background
  exchange.
- problem it tried to solve: replace vague phase-transfer language with a
  runnable object.
- what survived: retained `Gamma(a)` exposed timing constraints and ruled out
  early-time transfer profiles in earlier scans.
- what was demoted: retained `Gamma(a)` is a prototype phenomenological transfer
  profile, not a derived physical law.
- what was rejected: do not justify the current `Gamma(a)` retroactively as
  microphysics.
- what remains active: future work must decide whether `Gamma(a)` is retained as
  an IV/IDE timing prior, derived from a source scalar, lifted into `Q^nu`,
  constrained by `delta Q`, or discarded.

## Collapsed-Fraction And Information-Production Proxies

- idea introduced: structure-era proxies such as `dF_coll/dln(a)` might shape
  the transfer timing.
- problem it tried to solve: connect phase-transfer timing to collapse,
  information production, or source-like structure histories.
- what survived: the structure-era timing fingerprint remains a weak
  phenomenological feature.
- what was demoted: collapse or information-production timing is provenance and
  phenomenology, not an accepted physical source.
- what was rejected: the retained collapse/information-production source
  relation failed physical Level 1.5 promotion.
- what remains active: timing may still be tested as an IV/IDE
  prior-compression target under stronger evidence.

## Phenomenological IV/IDE Demotion

- idea introduced: the retained branch can be classified as interacting vacuum /
  time-dependent interacting dark energy under the declared closure.
- problem it tried to solve: identify whether the retained branch is independent
  physical QFUDS or belongs to a known model family.
- what survived: P1 survives only as Level 2A phenomenological
  interacting-vacuum closure.
- what was demoted: retained P1 is not an independent physical QFUDS branch.
- what was rejected: P2 regularized-fluid interpretation failed at retained
  amplitude.
- what remains active: phenomenological IV/IDE positioning, timing-prior tests,
  and reduction comparisons.

## Blocked Physical-QFUDS Level 2B Status

- idea introduced: Physical-QFUDS Level 2B requires a physical perturbation
  closure, not only a stable phenomenological closure.
- problem it tried to solve: prevent background or Level 2A survival from being
  overstated as physical theory.
- what survived: a clear admission rule for future physical branches.
- what was demoted: current retained-branch status remains phenomenological.
- what was rejected: no Physical-QFUDS Level 2B work is allowed from retained
  `Gamma(a)` timing alone.
- what remains active: a future physical branch may be admitted only if one
  mechanism supplies `X`, `Q^nu`, phase-B vacuum-pressure rationale, `delta Q`,
  and known-model distinction.

## What Remains Active

- two-effective-phase bookkeeping as a controlled model language;
- P1 as Level 2A phenomenological interacting-vacuum closure;
- structure-era timing as a possible IV/IDE prior-compression target;
- safe future audits for black-hole-coupled sources, HDE reduction, DM-only
  remnants, and causal-set Lambda comparison;
- the broader physical-QFUDS question, only if a future mechanism satisfies the
  admission rule.

## What Was Demoted Or Rejected

- Landauer, information conservation, black-hole information, white-hole,
  remnant, and generic foam language are motivation or provenance unless turned
  into equations and observables.
- The retained collapse/information-production `Gamma(a)` branch failed
  physical Level 1.5 promotion.
- Retained P1 is classified as phenomenological IV/IDE, not independent physical
  QFUDS.
- P2 failed at retained amplitude.
- Level 2B remains blocked.
