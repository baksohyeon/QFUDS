# Cohen Kaplan Nelson 1999 EFT Black Holes Cosmological Constant

## Bibliographic Metadata

- Paper: "Effective Field Theory, Black Holes, and the Cosmological Constant"
- Authors: Andrew G. Cohen, David B. Kaplan, Ann E. Nelson
- arXiv: [hep-th/9803132](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9803132)
- Journal reference: Phys. Rev. Lett. 82, 4971 (1999)

## Key Equations

- Proposed CKN bound: UV and IR cutoffs in effective field theory cannot be
  independent in the presence of gravity; the largest allowed vacuum energy is
  bounded by the energy of a black hole of the same size as the IR cutoff.
- Resulting relation often written as ρ_Λ ≲ M_pl^2 / L^2 where L is the IR
  cutoff length scale.

## QFUDS Relevance

- Foundational reference for the holographic dark energy (HDE) framework,
  reframed in CP25 S4 as the academic locus for the user intuition "use an IR
  cutoff at galaxy / structure scale instead of a Planck UV cutoff".
- Provides the upper bound that subsequent HDE variants (Li 2004 future-horizon
  HDE, Cai 2007 agegraphic, Gao 2009 Ricci, Barrow, Tsallis) attempt to saturate
  with different L choices.

## Use Restrictions

- Do not treat the CKN bound as a derivation of dark energy. It is an
  effective-field-theory bound; the IR cutoff length L remains a chosen scale,
  not a derived foam-microphysics quantity.
- Do not infer QFUDS support from this record. The CKN bound is a comparator
  for the structure of the cosmological constant problem only.
