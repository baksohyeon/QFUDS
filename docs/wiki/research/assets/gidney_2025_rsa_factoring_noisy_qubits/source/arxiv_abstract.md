## Bibliographic

- Title: How to factor 2048 bit RSA integers with less than a million noisy qubits
- Author: Craig Gidney (Google Quantum AI)
- Submitted: 2025-05-21 (v1)
- Category: quant-ph

## Abstract (summary as captured)

Gidney reduces the qubit resource estimate for factoring a 2048-bit RSA integer:
fewer than one million noisy physical qubits, running in under a week. This is a
roughly order-of-magnitude reduction from the author's 2019 estimate of 20 million
qubits in 8 hours. The reduction comes from three techniques: approximate residue
arithmetic, yoked surface codes for storing idle logical qubits, and magic state
cultivation. Runtime increases relative to the 2019 estimate, but the work reports
over a 100-fold reduction in Toffoli gate count relative to a 2024 comparative
study.
