# 2026-06-10 Black-Hole-Coupled Source Audit

## Scope

This document executes the accepted
[2026-06-10 Black-Hole-Coupled Source Audit Plan](../plans/020_phase2_black_hole_coupled_source_audit_plan.md).

It asks whether any black-hole-related physical quantity can serve as a
meaningful source `X` for QFUDS, or whether the lane reduces to existing
black-hole-coupled dark energy, cosmologically coupled black-hole,
interacting-vacuum, holographic, or remnant-DM model families.

This audit was initially executed as a local repository audit. A follow-up web
literature search was then cached in
[2026-06-10 Black-Hole-Coupled Literature Search Audit](../coverage/022_phase2_black_hole_coupled_literature_search_audit.md).
The follow-up adds known-model comparators and constraints, but does not change
the admission result.

A broader non-black-hole literature expansion is recorded in
[2026-06-10 Structure-Era Activation Literature Audit](../coverage/024_phase2_structure_era_activation_literature_audit.md).
That expansion does not weaken this audit's black-hole-lane result, but it does
weaken any broader reading that the source literature is absent. The black-hole
negative result is therefore dated and scope-limited, not a global claim about
structure-era, backreaction, vacuum-activation, or nonparametric IDE literature.

## Status Boundary

Current retained-branch classification:
Phenomenological IV/IDE; Not Yet A Physical Model.

This classification applies to the current retained branch only. It does not define the entire QFUDS research program.

Gamma(a) is a prototype phenomenological transfer profile, not a derived physical law.

The black-hole-coupled source audit must not prove Gamma(a). It must determine whether any black-hole-related source X can replace, derive, constrain, or reject it.

This audit does not:

- open Physical-QFUDS [Level 2B](../../../../glossary/repository_levels.md);
- modify roadmap status;
- create a black-hole physical branch;
- claim black holes explain `Gamma(a)`;
- claim black-hole entropy explains phase B;
- claim remnants explain dark energy;
- equate information preservation with vacuum pressure.

## Candidate Quantity Findings

The audit evaluates concrete source quantities only. Model families such as
black-hole-coupled dark energy and cosmologically coupled black-hole models are
used only as reduction-test comparators.

| Candidate quantity | Audit finding |
| --- | --- |
| black-hole production history | Not admitted. A production rate can be named, but the repository has no source history, normalization, stress-energy transfer, phase-B pressure rationale, or `delta Q` route. |
| black-hole mass growth history | Not admitted. This is the strongest quantity-level candidate because it overlaps cosmologically coupled black-hole ideas, but external comparator literature does not turn mass growth into QFUDS phase transfer. |
| black-hole accretion history | Not admitted. Accretion histories are ordinary astrophysical growth or radiative feedback unless a separate stress-energy relation maps them into phase B. |
| black-hole merger history | Not admitted. Merger history mainly tracks compact-object bookkeeping and does not by itself define smooth vacuum-pressure energy. |
| total black-hole entropy `S_BH` | Not admitted. External entropy/topological dark-energy literature exists, but generic `S_BH` accounting is not a QFUDS stress-energy transfer law. |
| `dS_BH / dln(a)` | Not admitted. This could be a sharper timing proxy if computed from a real mass/accretion/merger history, but it still lacks QFUDS `Q^nu`, phase-B pressure, and `delta Q`. |
| black-hole remnant contribution | DM-only or defect-sector audit only. External remnant/white-hole dark-matter literature exists; it is not a dark-energy source unless smooth `w ~= -1` stress-energy is derived. |

| Comparator family | Reduction-test result |
| --- | --- |
| black-hole-coupled dark energy | High reduction risk. If black-hole production or mass growth sources the DE density without a distinct QFUDS transfer equation, the lane is black-hole-coupled DE, not physical QFUDS. |
| cosmologically coupled black-hole models | High reduction risk. This is the closest model-family neighbor for mass-growth and coupled-vacuum ideas, but repository evidence does not extract a QFUDS-specific `X`, `Q^nu`, or `delta Q`. |

The strongest reduction-test result is negative: the black-hole-coupled lane is
scientifically meaningful as a source audit, but current repository evidence
does not distinguish it from known black-hole-coupled DE, IV/IDE with a chosen
history, or compact-remnant models.

## Failure Criteria Results

The lane fails admission for the current repository state because it has only:

- named candidate histories, not an accepted source equation;
- real entropy objects, but no entropy-to-stress-energy transfer law;
- possible timing variables, but no derivation of retained `Gamma(a)`;
- no covariant `Q^nu`;
- no reason phase B has smooth `w ~= -1`;
- no physical `delta Q` route;
- no distinct observable against black-hole-coupled DE or cosmologically coupled
  black-hole models;
- no QFUDS-ready black-hole source-history product, even after the follow-up
  literature cache pass.

Do not treat black-hole timing similarity as sufficient.

Do not treat black-hole information or entropy language as sufficient.

Do not treat black-hole-coupled dark energy literature as QFUDS success unless
QFUDS has a distinct equation, observable, or falsifiable prediction.

## What Counts As Progress

Progress would be any one of the following audit outputs, without opening Level
2B:

- a real black-hole production, mass-growth, accretion, merger, entropy, or
  remnant data product added to the literature cache;
- a non-circular source scalar with units and normalization;
- an explicit reduction result showing the lane is just black-hole-coupled DE or
  cosmologically coupled black-hole phenomenology;
- a falsifiable difference between QFUDS and the closest known model family;
- a predeclared perturbation route for `delta Q`.

The follow-up literature cache improves comparator coverage and removes the
earlier repository-cache gap for the broad topic. It does not remove the actual
QFUDS evidence gap: no cached external source provides a non-circular QFUDS
source scalar, normalization, `Q^nu`, phase-B stress-energy rationale,
`delta Q`, and distinct QFUDS observable together.

## Executive Decision

Decision: retain the black-hole-coupled lane as a safe future audit lane only.

No candidate quantity currently supplies a physically meaningful source `X` for
QFUDS. The most promising quantity-level directions are black-hole mass growth
history and `dS_BH/dln(a)`, but both remain incomplete because they lack
normalization, `Q^nu`, phase-B vacuum-pressure rationale, `delta Q`, and
known-model distinction.

Current best classification:

- black-hole mass growth: strongest future source audit candidate, not admitted;
- `dS_BH/dln(a)`: strongest entropy-timing candidate, not admitted;
- production, accretion, and merger histories: source-audit candidates, not
  admitted;
- total `S_BH`: real thermodynamics, not a transfer law;
- entropy/topological dark-energy papers: external comparators, not QFUDS
  admission evidence;
- remnants: DM-only or defect-sector audit, not DE.

Expanded-literature classification:

- A. robust after expanded literature search for the black-hole-coupled lane
  only;
- B. weakened if read as a global negative result for all possible Source-X
  literature;
- C. redirected from black-hole source toward structure-era activation for the
  next broad audit lane;
- D. still unresolved for physical admission because data products and
  admission-rule items remain missing.
