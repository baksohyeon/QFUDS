## Episode 1 : The One-Way Door

> Meta (production only) · New concept: "the one-way lock broke. Send and the key
> is copied; hold and it is gone." · Onboarding: problem-first + analogy + short
> accurate note (018 B1). · Reveal budget: company and machine identity hidden,
> mechanism only. · Hook: he runs verification and the bar goes backward.

### Storyboard

**Panel 1 · The laugh**
- Frame: thumbs scrolling. A screenshot of a wallet hitting zero the moment it moved.
- Caption: "lost his keys, lol. his own fault."
- SFX: notifications, ping, ping.
- Beat: someone else's problem.

**Panel 2 · The pattern**
- Frame: only the wallets that sent are empty. The ones left alone are fine.
- Caption (to self): "...only the senders?"
- Beat: the laughing stops.

**Panel 3 · The fine print (in-world doc)**
- Frame: under the send button, a folded warning line he never read.
- Caption: "Sending publishes your signature, and your public key, to the ledger."
- Beat: yesterday it was boilerplate.

**Panel 4 · The one-way rule (accurate + analogy)**
- Frame: a lock (the public address) and a key (the secret).
- Caption: everyone sees the address. Only the owner holds the key. The address never made the key. Forward had a road. Backward had none.
- Beat: that one fact was why the world felt safe.

**Panel 5 · The broken promise (pressure)**
- Frame: now someone walks the road backward, cutting the key from the address alone.
- Caption: that is why the senders went first. Send, the address surfaces; the address surfaces, the key is copied.
- Beat: the trap takes shape.

**Panel 6 · His night (catch-22)**
- Frame: one person, one wallet, one payment due by morning.
- Caption: send and you are robbed. Hold and it is gone by morning. A finger stops over the button.
- SFX: heartbeat.
- Beat: neither way is a way.

**Panel 7 · Handed down**
- Frame (cut): morning. The money leaves his hands as a single file in a company's cold room.
- Caption: sixteen degrees. The man at the desk gets only the file. He never sees who stood behind it.
- Beat: a weight passed on.

**Panel 8 · The lowest seat**
- Frame: Sael, coat still on, at the lowest desk in the room.
- Caption: his job is one thing. Decide if a case is real, and pass it up.
- Beat: just get through today.

**Panel 9 · Two lines that meet**
- Frame: the seats above are dark. A case that should never reach him is on his screen.
- Caption: "signature checks, ledger checks, the owner sent it." / "I never sent it."
- Beat: this is not my case.

**Panel 10 · The bar runs backward (hook)**
- Frame: he pulls the case to his seat and runs verification. The progress bar moves the wrong way.
- Caption: the machine stops matching the signature and starts tracing which key it came from.
- SFX: a low machine hum.
- Beat: a door meant to open forward opens in reverse.

### Prose

At first, everyone laughed.

Someone moved their coins and the balance hit zero in the same breath. The replies
came fast. Scam. Phishing. What you get for not minding your keys. A stranger's
problem, one thumb-flick away.

Then the same post came again. And again. Only the wallets that had sent were
emptied. The ones left untouched were fine. Press send, and after the press,
nothing. By then, no one was laughing.

He was reading the thread with his own wallet open. There was one payment he had
to push through by morning. Not a fortune. It just happened to be the only money
sitting in that wallet and nowhere else. His bank worked. His card worked. He
could still buy coffee. Only that one transfer would not move.

Under the send button, folded small, was a warning line he had never once read.
Sending publishes your signature to the ledger, along with your public key.
Until yesterday it had been boilerplate.

To move the money he had to sign with a secret key. The instant he signed, the
wallet's public lock would surface on a ledger anyone could read. Picture the
public key as a street address and the private key as the key to the door. Until
yesterday you could study the address all you liked and never cut the key.
Forward had a road. Backward had none. Every wallet in the world leaned on that
one fact.

And now someone had found the road back. From the address alone they were filing
the shape of the key. That was why the senders went first. Send, and the address
surfaced; the address surfaced, and the key was copied.

Send, and you were robbed. Hold, and no one could promise the money would still
be there by morning. To run, you had to open the door, and opening it let
everyone watch you do it.

He set a finger on send. He could not press it. He could not lift it.

By morning the money had left his hands anyway. It came into a company's cold
room as a single file. The room was kept at sixteen degrees. The man at the desk
received only the file. He never saw who had stood behind it.

At the lowest seat in that room sat Sael.

His work was simple. Decide whether a case was real, then pass it up. The verdict
was never his. That distance had kept him safe.

That day the seats above him were dark. Something had closed the floor the night
before, and only a few hands were left at the bottom. A case that would never have
reached him reached him.

Two lines stood against each other on the file. One said the signature checked,
the ledger checked, the owner had sent it himself. The other said: I never sent
it. I never pressed anything.

Until yesterday, those two lines never met on the same page.

Sael read them twice. Then he pulled the case to his own seat. To see what was
real, he had to ask the machine first.

### Hook

He sent the transaction to be verified. Verification ran one way. Match the
signature against the public key, and done.

The bar did not go that way. Instead of matching the signature, the machine
started to walk backward, tracing which key it had come from.

A door that was only ever meant to open forward was, on his desk, sliding open
in reverse.
