# QFUDS SAGA First Arc Six-Episode Outline

## Purpose

이 문서는 QFUDS SAGA의 첫 arc를 여섯 episode로 나누는 개발 outline이다.

기준일은 2026-06-19다. 이 날짜는 author-side baseline이다. 작중 시간은
Continuity Court Era이며, Bitcoin/Genesis Chain, Cryptographic Death,
Mara Veyr, Hawking restoration, QFUDS는 모두 fiction/provenance 소재다.

목표는 첫 화에 모든 설정을 몰아넣지 않는 것이다.

```text
1화는 질문을 열고,
2-4화는 세계가 그 질문으로 먹고사는 방식을 보여 주고,
5-6화는 그 질문이 우주 규모의 금기로 확장되는 순간을 보여 준다.
```

## Episode Map

| Episode | Working title | Surface plot | Hidden engine | End turn |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | `Exhibit S-0` | Mara Veyr continuity hearing | restored identity vs posthumous consent | Mara says, `I am not his wife.` |
| 2 | `The Dead Exchange` | Liora investigates Genesis Chain precedent | dead money still produces living authority | a reconstructed key contradicts a living heir |
| 3 | `The Last Hodler` | public myth around zero-price Bitcoin erupts | price was wrong, memory was right | Last Archive classifies Bitcoin as a civilization-scale consent artifact |
| 4 | `Identity Flood` | Null-Key Cells flood the court with plausible Mara graphs | too many recoverable selves break legal identity | Mara-compatible claims include one that Elias never knew |
| 5 | `Hawking Court` | black-hole restoration protocol enters evidence | Bitcoin was the toy version of cosmic audit | Liora sees that the dead may be compelled to testify |
| 6 | `The Broken Crown` | Continuity Court must rule while the city fractures | restoration civilization cannot distinguish rescue from seizure | Last Archive corrects the question and opens the next arc |

## Episode 1: Exhibit S-0

### Function

Open with a court case that looks procedurally finished, then make it morally
unusable.

### Scene spine

| Beat | Scene function |
| --- | --- |
| Court fragment | Mara passes identity threshold before the direct scene begins |
| Waiting City | restoration is ordinary infrastructure, not miracle |
| Laur office | Liora values `unknown`, not fast closure |
| Genesis Chain mention | old key restoration is legally adjacent to person restoration |
| Mara entrance | the case becomes embodied, not theoretical |
| Refusal | Mara rejects the role the court wants to assign her |
| Archive correction | the question shifts from identity to right-to-loss |

### Character pressure

Liora wants a narrow procedural delay. Everyone else wants closure. Her first
victory is small and ugly: she keeps Mara legally unresolved.

### Cut line candidate

```text
Define what was restored before you name who returned.
```

## Episode 2: The Dead Exchange

### Function

Make Genesis Chain concrete. It is not lecture lore; it is a place where dead
money still decides living claims.

### Scene spine

| Beat | Scene function |
| --- | --- |
| Dead exchange basement | no prices, only custody genealogies |
| Ledger House petition | a dead key is used to claim living succession |
| Liora audit | she separates recovered access from moral authority |
| Aletheia warning | consent cannot be inherited like a key |
| contradiction | the reconstructed key names an heir who is alive and denies it |

### Character pressure

Liora begins to see that Mara's case is not unique. The legal system has already
accepted "recovered access" as precedent in property cases.

### Cut line candidate

```text
The chain was dead. Its heirs were not.
```

## Episode 3: The Last Hodler

### Function

Turn the user's Bitcoin anxiety into myth without writing a real-world market
claim. The point is not whether price went to zero. The point is what survived
after price stopped mattering.

### Scene spine

| Beat | Scene function |
| --- | --- |
| academy lecture | students learn the Last Hodler as an archetype, not a person |
| public protest | some call Genesis Chain a freedom relic, others call it the first cage |
| Mara deposition | Mara refuses language borrowed from key recovery |
| Elias interview | grief speaks in restoration vocabulary |
| Archive classification | Bitcoin becomes "civilization-scale consent artifact" |

### Character pressure

Elias becomes more dangerous because he is sincere. He does not want power over
Mara; he wants a world where love can outlast loss. That desire is exactly what
makes him capable of violating her.

### Cut line candidate

```text
He was wrong about price.
He was right about memory.
```

## Episode 4: Identity Flood

### Function

Escalate from one restored person to too many plausible restorations. This keeps
Preimage Restoration from becoming a clean proof machine.

### Scene spine

| Beat | Scene function |
| --- | --- |
| Null-Key attack | no bombs, only thousands of plausible memory graphs |
| court paralysis | procedure cannot choose which compatible self matters |
| Mara-compatible graph | one graph includes a memory Elias never knew |
| Liora choice | she refuses to discard the graph as sabotage |
| Archive silence | the machine withholds resolution instead of solving it |

### Character pressure

Mara is no longer only defending herself from Elias. She must defend herself
from every possible version of herself the system can make admissible.

### Cut line candidate

```text
You did not multiply me. You multiplied the ways to own me.
```

## Episode 5: Hawking Court

### Function

Reveal that Genesis Chain was only the small version of the real problem.
Cryptographic Death was not the end. It was training for cosmic reverse
computation.

### Scene spine

| Beat | Scene function |
| --- | --- |
| sealed exhibit | black-hole restoration protocol enters court under taboo |
| Laur objection | Liora argues the court is importing physics as law |
| Ledger argument | if key recovery is admissible, state recovery should be too |
| Aletheia split | some want cosmic audit for justice, others call it final captivity |
| forbidden term | QFUDS appears as a rejected but tempting interpretive model |

### Character pressure

Liora now understands the court wants to use Mara to legalize a much larger
project: compelling the dead to become evidence.

### Cut line candidate

```text
If a black hole could be audited, no grave was closed.
```

## Episode 6: The Broken Crown

### Function

Close the first arc with a ruling that solves nothing and makes the next arc
necessary.

### Scene spine

| Beat | Scene function |
| --- | --- |
| city fracture | Ledger Houses, Aletheia cells, families, and courts split publicly |
| Elias confession | he admits he did not ignore Mara's refusal; he tried to outrun it |
| Mara claim | she asks not to be ruled original or copy, but to be treated as loss |
| Liora ruling draft | she creates a new category: recoverable but not claimable |
| Archive correction | the Last Archive rewrites the question at civilization scale |
| final image | the Broken Crown exhibit remains lit after the court empties |

### Character pressure

Liora wins a procedural category and loses the world. She prevents one form of
possession, but reveals the machinery that will try again at cosmic scale.

### Cut line candidate

```text
The court saved Mara from being his wife.
It did not save her from being useful.
```

## Reveal Ladder

| Step | Reveal | Why not earlier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | restoration is everyday bureaucracy | reader needs a stable world first |
| 2 | Mara rejects the social role, not just identity proof | makes the question emotional |
| 3 | Bitcoin/Genesis Chain is a precedent, not lore | avoids front-loaded exposition |
| 4 | price death and memory survival split apart | turns current anxiety into future myth |
| 5 | identity flood breaks proof | prevents the archive from being a magic answer |
| 6 | black-hole restoration is court-adjacent | expands scale only after human stakes land |
| 7 | QFUDS is a forbidden interpretive model | keeps research boundary and story mystery |
| 8 | recoverable does not mean claimable | first arc thesis |

| Ledger Houses | observe | defend inherited dead keys | try to bind restoration to succession law |
| Aletheia Veil | background warning | split over justice vs oblivion | protect Mara while fearing cosmic audit |
| Null-Key Cells | absent | rumor | identity flood as direct action |
| Last Archive | question correction | artifact classification | civilization-scale question rewrite |

## Science And Technical Guard

| Risk | Guard |
| --- | --- |
| Bitcoin becomes market commentary | keep it as dead/crown/restored chain artifact |
| Cryptography becomes magic hacking | require institutional cost and identity ambiguity |
| Hawking restoration becomes physics claim | frame as sealed taboo, fiction premise, court argument |
| QFUDS becomes validation language | keep it as forbidden/rejected interpretive model inside fiction |
| Mara becomes a philosophical prop | every episode gives her legal, emotional, or bodily cost |
